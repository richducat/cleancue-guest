Privacy Policy

Effective July 17, 2026 • Private pilot

iPhone and Android web storage

Imported audio and full lyric transcriptions stay on your device. The Android DJ web pilot stores tracks, events, device-preview requests, and emcee notes in that browser’s local storage. CleanCue does not upload audio for analysis.

What the pilot service stores

If you connect pilot sync after it is activated, CleanCue may store your email address, events, emcee notes, compact track-analysis summaries, guest song requests, feedback, and limited product-use events. Track summaries may include title, artist, BPM, key, confidence, and explicit-category counts—but not audio or full transcripts.

Guest requests

Guests may provide a song, artist, name, and message. Device-preview requests remain in the same browser. When the cross-device pilot service is activated, it creates a short-lived one-way hash from network and browser information for spam rate limiting and does not retain the raw IP address in the application database.

Deletion

Android web users can choose “Delete my browser data” on the Library screen. iPhone users can delete local and synced account data inside Pilot Settings. Clearing browser site data also removes the Android pilot’s local content.

Tracking and accuracy

CleanCue contains no ads, does not track you across apps or websites, and does not sell personal information. Automated lyric, BPM, and key analysis may be wrong; review results before relying on them at an event.

Questions are handled through the support page.
