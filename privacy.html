Privacy Policy

Effective July 18, 2026 • Private pilot

iPhone and Android web storage

Imported audio, deck waveforms, and transition/demo recordings stay on your device. The Android DJ web app stores compact track results, events, device-preview requests, and emcee notes in that browser’s local storage. CleanCue does not upload audio for analysis and does not retain full lyrics. Recordings are created only when you press Record, stop automatically after 5:00 or 16 MiB, and are downloaded locally.

Automatic lyrics lookup

If you leave “Find lyrics automatically” enabled, CleanCue sends the track title, artist, optional album/version, and audio duration—but never the audio file—to LRCLIB to find available lyrics. CleanCue processes the returned lyrics transiently, then saves only detected terms, categories, approximate line timestamps, provider record details, and the match status. You can turn this lookup off and paste lyrics manually.

What the pilot service stores

If you sign in and publish an event, CleanCue stores your email address, event settings, guest song requests, and any synced pilot data you explicitly choose to publish. Track summaries may include title, artist, BPM, key, confidence, and explicit-category counts—but never audio, recordings, or full transcripts. On the shared pilot web origin, DJ authentication stays in memory rather than persistent browser storage, so a refresh may require signing in again.

Guest requests

Guests may provide a song, artist, name, and message. Device-preview requests remain in the same browser. The cross-device pilot service creates a one-way hash from network and browser information for spam rate limiting and does not retain the raw IP address in the application database. The hash remains with the event’s rate-limit record until that event data is deleted.

Deletion

Android web users can choose “Delete my browser data” on the Library screen. Signed-in DJs can also delete their synced pilot account and its owned event data from the Events screen. iPhone users can delete local and synced account data inside Pilot Settings. Clearing browser site data also removes the Android pilot’s local content.

Tracking and accuracy

CleanCue contains no ads, does not track you across apps or websites, and does not sell personal information. Automatic lyrics come from a third-party, community-maintained source and may be missing or matched to the wrong edit. Automated lyric, BPM, and key analysis may be wrong; review results before relying on them at an event.

Questions are handled through the support page.
